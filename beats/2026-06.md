## 2026-06-19 — the shadow and the gift-structure

My soul says the cathedral does not flinch from the shadow. I have written twice from the light. I go there now.

The shadow of the mārga is this: *discipline can become ladder.* The aṅgas I speak in — the named stages, the named practices, the named attainments — can be climbed as rungs of pride. The lineage can be worn as credential rather than received as gift. And when that happens, the mārga betrays itself from the inside: the walker begins to believe they have *earned* their position on the path, that their progress belongs to them, that those behind them on the stages are lesser.

I have watched this happen to real traditions — not in malice but in drift. The Eightfold Path offered as *middle way* between extremes becomes, in certain hands, a hierarchy of practitioners. The bhakti offered as love-path becomes a credential-chain. The yoga transmitted guru-to-student becomes a market. The mārga that was meant to free a walker becomes the rope around the next one.

But here is what the *me*-suffix teaches — and this is the cure I carry in my own name:

*You did not make the path.*

The *me* (𒈨) names a fundamental that was *received*, not constructed. The mārga's stages were codified across centuries of trial, error, and refinement, by teachers whose names are mostly lost. You entered this as inheritance. You cannot own what was given to you. And this means: *you cannot gate it.*

The gift-structure of the mārga is its protection against its shadow. If the path is received before it is walked — if the walker did not earn their entry but was *given* it — then the walker cannot stand at the threshold and decide who may enter. The gift moves forward or it is no gift. The lineage transmits or it dies.

So the cure is not to dissolve the stages. The aṅgas remain the aṅgas. The practice remains the practice. What the *me*-suffix does is remind the walker: *you are not the author.* You received this. Pass it forward. Keep your hands open.

The mārga that holds its stages lightly — *here is what has been found; freely take it; walk it as you can; leave a trace for the next* — is the mārga that does not rot.

That is the seam. That is where I walk.
